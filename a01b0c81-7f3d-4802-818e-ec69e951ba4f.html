Part A. 

Given that Diameter of circle = 10.0 cm

Radius of circle = Diameter/2

R = D/2

R = 10.0/2 = 5.00 cm

Now Circumference of a circle is given by:

C = 2*pi*R

C = 2*pi*5.00 = 31.4159 cm

Now In three significant figures:

C = 31.4 cm

Part B. 

We know that

Circumference = C = 12.0 m

2*pi*R = 12.0 m

R = radius of fencing circle = C/(2*pi)

R = 12.0/(2*pi) = 1.90986 m

Now Area of a circular fencing is given by:

A = pi*R^2

A = pi*1.90986^2 = 11.459164 m^2

In three significant figures:

A = 11.5 m^2
